## Design Context

### Users
Self-hosters, small teams, and households organizing physical storage. They're technical enough to run Docker but not designers. They use the product in garages, workshops, shared spaces — often on phones with dirty hands or at a desk planning an organization session. The blog audience is slightly broader: people searching for QR inventory, home organization, open source storage tools.

### Brand Personality
**Practical, honest, sharp.** No-nonsense tool that respects your time. Gets out of the way. Says what it does in plain language, proves it with the product, and doesn't oversell. Copy is direct and conversational — short sentences, specific claims ("One Docker container" not "easy deployment").

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dense and fast like Linear/Raycast, but with the warmth and rounded friendliness of Notion/Arc. Not cold or sterile — approachable but competent.
- **Theme**: Dark mode only. Users are often in garages, workshops, or dimly lit spaces.
- **Color**: Purple brand (#5e2fe0) as accent. Flat surfaces — no blur, no shadows. Solid borders (1px divider).
- **Typography**: DM Sans 700 for headings (tight tracking), Inter (VitePress default) for body. No gradient text.
- **Motion**: Exponential ease-out (cubic-bezier 0.16, 1, 0.3, 1). Entrance animations on load, scroll-reveal below fold. Respects prefers-reduced-motion.
- **References**: Linear (sharp dark UI, dense information), Raycast (fast, developer-focused), Notion (warm rounded elements), Arc (sophisticated but friendly).
- **Anti-references**: Generic SaaS hero templates (Vercel starter look), Dev.to/Medium engagement-bait social, overly minimal text-only blogs.

### Design Principles
1. **Flat and honest** — No shadows, no blur, no gradients on surfaces. Solid borders, solid colors. What you see is what it is.
2. **Dense but breathing** — Pack information efficiently (Linear-style) but give sections room to breathe (generous between groups, tight within).
3. **Sharp typography** — DM Sans headings with tight tracking create the "sharp" personality. Body text stays readable at 15-16px.
4. **Purple as punctuation** — Brand purple is an accent, not a theme. Use sparingly for interactive elements, active states, and key CTAs. Everything else is neutral.
5. **Content earns its space** — No decorative elements that don't serve the content. No hero images for the sake of hero images. If a cover image adds context, show it. If not, skip it.
